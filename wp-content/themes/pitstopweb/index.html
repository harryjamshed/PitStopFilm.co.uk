SYNOPSIS

Pit Stop is a dark drama that focusses on the struggle of 22 year old ‘Noveed’; an immigrant working and living at a hand car wash alongside his depressive and violent father - a man hell-bent on ensuring Noveed doesn’t neglect his heritage.

 

Over the course of one night and one terrifying, forced meeting with a pushy, psychotic customer - Noveed is met with an opportunity that could change his life forever.

 

Pit stop is the second short film written and directed by Jack King. It was produced by Garry Paton of Finite Productions and was shot on location at Whinmoor hand car wash in West Yorkshire. The film was completed in August 2015 and has begun its festival run. Please check back soon for more information or follow us on Facebook

FESTIVALS / SCREENINGS

Pit Stop has been selected for the London Short Film Festival 2016 and will screen in January. Screening information will be updated when the programme is released and more festival selections are announced.

Aqib Khan as Noveed

Aqib had his screen debut in the 2010 film West Is West, the sequel to the 1999 film East Is East. He was just 15 years old when the film was released and was considered “something of a find” for the film due to his resemblance to actor Jordan Routledge who played the role of Sajid in the original film. His work on the film received critical acclaim. Since filming, Aqib has gone on to work with some top names in television and film starring alongside Julie Walters in The Jury, and Michael Palin in Remember Me. He is currently shooting a feature film with Harvey Keitel and Gabriel Byrne in Yorkshire for release next year.

Douglas Russell as Ewen

Douglas has recently returned from Bulgaria after shooting October Film’s Barbarians Rising, playing a warrior, fighting against the Roman Empire. This follows, playing Axel, for the forthcoming ITV network series Beowolf. Other recent credits include Lennox, in the Sony / Starz period epic Outlander, Family Portrait, BBC’s Waterloo’s Warriors, BBC’s Eve, The Survivalist, Let Us Prey and The Wee Man as well as Peter Mullens' Neds. Douglas also enjoys a stage and radio drama career, plays the guitar and drums.

Javed Khan as Hasan

Javed Khan is a British actor based in London. His many TV credits include: Adha Cup (Channel 4), Hotel Babylon 3 and Holby Blue and film credits: Pack of Pain (Award Winner of Phoenix Comicon ‘Best action film’), Welcome to London, Musafir (Nominated Best UK Short at Raindance), Two Dosas, Romeo Vs Juliet and My Beautiful White Skin. Javed will be filming the lead in a new feature film in 2016 and can be seen next spring on BBC One in the docu-drama Lawfull Killing. His most recent theatre was Battlebus (Old Red Lion) and Broken Dolls (New York).

Jack King - Writer/Director

Jack is a self-taught film-maker from Bradford, Yorkshire - cutting his teeth making narrative music videos for a variety of artists and major record labels, from universal to EMI, and in 2012 being signed to world leading agency ‘colonel blimp’. However over the last few years, Jack has turned his attentions to his first passion - writing and developing shorts, self-funding his first short ‘Geezer’, which won best Drama at the isle of wight film festival, as well as picking up selections around the country, before writing and directing Pit-stop. In May 2016 Jack shot his first 10 minute off-beat comedy 'Something beginning with P' in St helens, which is currently in post-production. In the meantime Jack has returned to working independently on music videos, represented by OB management in London. You can see more of Jacks work on his website KingJack.co.uk or visit his Vimeo

Garry Paton - Producer / Editor

Garry Paton is the creative director of Finite Productions – a production company based in Manchester which specialises in drama and online video content. Garry is a film producer and editor and has made a wealth of short films - some of which have gone on to compete at festivals such as the Edinburgh International Film Festival, the European Independent Film Festival and the London Short Film Festival. Garry also edits broadcast television and enjoys the process of storytelling through many different genres.

MIKO JAROSZEWICZ - D.O.P

A graduate of the Polish National Film School, Miko has been lighting for over fifteen years. He was DoP on the 2008 Bafta nominated and Oscar winning short ‘Peter and the Wolf’, and has worked on features, shorts, television programs and commercials here in the UK, Germany and in his native Poland. He is now based in London and available for work worldwide.
